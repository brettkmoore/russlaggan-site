Type 10: Strategist-Analyst

Conviction meets Structure

You make bold moves backed by bulletproof data. Your confidence is earned through exhaustive preparation.

Your Profile

Who You Are as an Agent

As a Strategist-Analyst hybrid, you combine the Strategist's boldness with the Analyst's preparation. This makes you a formidable negotiator and strategist—you take strong positions, but every position is backed by data that's nearly impossible to refute. You don't just have opinions; you have evidence.

Primary Type

The Strategist

Middle · Conviction

In real estate, Conviction closes deals. You're the agent who tells a seller their price is too high on day one, and they respect you for it. You negotiate hard because you actually believe in your position.

Secondary Type

The Analyst

Pointer · Structure

In real estate, Structure wins listings. Sellers choose you because your CMA is the most thorough they've ever seen, and buyers trust you because your process eliminates surprises.

Your Approach

How You Work with Clients

You lead with conviction—taking positions, making recommendations, driving decisions. But unlike a pure Strategist, your boldness is underpinned by rigorous analysis. Before you push for a price, you've run every comp. Before you challenge an appraisal, you've built a counter-report. This makes your confidence contagious because it's clearly earned.

Your Edge

Strengths & Watch-Outs

Strengths

Telling clients the hard truths that other agents avoid
Negotiating aggressively because you believe in your position
Creating urgency through directness and confidence
Standing out in a sea of "yes" agents by having real opinions

Watch-Outs

May alienate risk-averse clients with your intensity
Can create unnecessary friction by challenging everything
May struggle with clients who need gentle guidance
Best Fit

Your Ideal Client

Competitive sellers in hot markets, investors, and anyone who wants an agent who will fight for every dollar—and has the data to back up every move.

Your Superpower

In negotiations, you present a counter-offer with a detailed market analysis that reframes the entire conversation. The other side doesn't just accept your terms—they wonder if they underpriced their position.

Your Growth Edge

Your combination of boldness and data can make you intimidating. Not every client wants a general—some want a guide. Learning to modulate your intensity based on the client will expand your reach.
